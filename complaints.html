Complaints and Content Removal Policy

Last updated: April 24, 2026

SHADOWNET takes all reports of illegal content, violations of our Terms of Service, or violations of card-scheme Standards very seriously. This policy describes how any user, visitor, creator, or depicted person can report content and how we process those reports.

1. How to File a Complaint

You can submit a complaint through any of the following channels:

  • In-app Report button: every post, live stream, direct message, and profile has a Report icon. Tap it, select the category, and submit.
  • Website complaint form: send an email to info80806@gmail.com with the subject line "Complaint" and the URL or username in question.
  • CCBill complaint form: CCBill Complaints / Content Removal Request.
  • DMCA copyright claims: see our DMCA page.

2. What You Can Report

  • Content that depicts minors or suggests minors in any way (CSAM) — reported immediately to law enforcement and NCMEC.
  • Non-consensual content or content where consent is disputed.
  • Content you believe depicts you without your consent.
  • Content that is illegal in any jurisdiction (trafficking, violence, drugs, etc.).
  • Copyright infringement (see DMCA).
  • Content that violates our Terms of Service or card-scheme Standards.

3. Our Review Process

  • Acknowledgement: within 24 hours we confirm receipt of your report.
  • Investigation: our admin reviews the reported content against our Standards, our Terms of Service, and applicable law. We may contact the reporter, the creator, or the depicted person for additional information.
  • Resolution: all reported complaints are reviewed and resolved within 5 business days of acknowledgement.
  • Illegal content: if our investigation yields evidence of illegal content (including CSAM or trafficking), we remove it immediately and report to the appropriate authorities.

4. Possible Outcomes

  • Content left up — no violation found.
  • Content removed — violation confirmed; content is taken down.
  • Creator warned — minor or first-time violation; creator is notified.
  • Creator suspended — temporary account suspension pending further review.
  • Creator permanently banned — for severe or repeated violations.
  • Referral to authorities — where required by law.

5. Appeal Procedure

Any decision can be appealed. Any person who is depicted in content on SHADOWNET has an unconditional right to request the removal of that content. If the content provider disputes the removal, SHADOWNET applies the following appeal procedure:

  • The appealing party submits a written appeal to info80806@gmail.com within 10 business days of the original decision.
  • SHADOWNET re-reviews the matter within 5 business days.
  • If the depicted person and the content provider still disagree, the dispute is escalated to a neutral third-party arbitrator whose decision is final and binding on both parties, as required by the CCBill Acceptable Use Policy. SHADOWNET bears the cost of such arbitration.
  • While an appeal is pending, contested content remains offline.

6. Pre-Screening of Uploaded Content

All user-uploaded content (images, videos, live streams, audio) passes through an automated moderation pipeline before it becomes publicly visible. This pipeline includes:

  • AI-based NSFW and CSAM detection.
  • Automated hash-matching against known illegal-content databases.
  • Perceptual-hash duplicate detection (to catch re-uploads of removed content).
  • Text OCR and caption scanning for prohibited terms (trafficking, minors, non-consensual activity).

Content flagged at any stage is quarantined and reviewed manually by the platform admin before it is allowed to go live. Live streams are monitored in real time by the same pipeline and can be terminated automatically. All moderation actions are logged in an internal audit table.

7. Monthly Reporting

SHADOWNET reports to CCBill by the second Monday of every month with a list of any URLs that had content violations during the previous month, including: how many violations, how many complaints received, what type of violations, and what action was taken.
