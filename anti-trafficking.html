Anti-Human Trafficking Policy

Last updated: April 24, 2026

1. Zero-Tolerance Commitment

SHADOWNET has a zero-tolerance policy against all forms of human trafficking, sex trafficking, forced labor, physical abuse, and exploitation. We are committed to providing a safe platform where all content is created consensually by verified adults.

This policy applies to everyone who interacts with SHADOWNET: the operator, creators, subscribers, visitors, and any third party.

2. Prohibited Activity

The following activities are strictly prohibited on SHADOWNET and will result in immediate account termination, content removal, and report to law enforcement where applicable:

  • Any content depicting minors (persons under 18 years of age), including simulated, AI-generated, drawn, or animated depictions (CSAM).
  • Content involving persons who are trafficked, coerced, forced, threatened, blackmailed, or otherwise unable to give free, informed, and ongoing consent.
  • Content promoting, glorifying, enabling, or facilitating human or sex trafficking.
  • Content depicting physical abuse, non-consensual violence, or rape.
  • Recruitment, solicitation, or transportation of persons for commercial sexual exploitation.
  • Any use of SHADOWNET communication features to coordinate trafficking, smuggling, or exploitation.

3. Preventive Controls

SHADOWNET enforces the following measures to prevent trafficking and exploitation:

  • Creator KYC: every creator must submit a government-issued photo ID and a live selfie before posting. Age is confirmed ≥18 via OCR and facial matching.
  • Content Provider Agreement: every creator signs an electronic Content Provider Agreement that prohibits trafficking and requires written consent from all depicted persons.
  • Automated pre-screening: all uploaded content passes AI moderation (NSFW/CSAM detection, hash-matching against known illegal databases, OCR for prohibited terms) before publication.
  • Search-term restrictions: we do not permit site-search terms or marketing language that suggest CSAM, non-consensual activity, or trafficking.
  • Live-stream monitoring: live streams are scanned in real time and terminated automatically on violations.

4. Reporting Suspected Trafficking

If you believe you have witnessed human trafficking, exploitation, or abuse on SHADOWNET, please report it immediately:

  • On the platform: use the Report button on the content, stream, profile, or message.
  • By email: info80806@gmail.com — subject line "Anti-Trafficking".
  • CCBill complaint form: Complaints / Content Removal Request.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services. International trafficking helplines:

  • United States: National Human Trafficking Hotline — 1-888-373-7888, or text "HELP" to 233733.
  • European Union: 116 006 (EU victim support hotline).
  • Italy: Numero Verde Anti-Tratta — 800-290-290.
  • International: Polaris Project.

5. Cooperation with Law Enforcement

SHADOWNET cooperates fully with law enforcement and authorized agencies investigating human trafficking, exploitation, or abuse. Upon receipt of a valid legal request (subpoena, court order, or equivalent instrument under applicable law), we will preserve and produce relevant records in our custody, including account information, uploaded content, and access logs.

In cases involving suspected minors or imminent harm, SHADOWNET will proactively contact the relevant authority (including the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)) without waiting for a request.

6. Training and Internal Compliance

The operator of SHADOWNET reviews this Anti-Human Trafficking Policy and all moderation procedures at least annually, and whenever there are material changes to our platform, processing partners, or applicable law. We track emerging trafficking tactics and adapt our pre-screening pipeline accordingly.
